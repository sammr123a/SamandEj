Tupac (2Pac) Shakur

Name/s: Tupac Amaru Shakur (full stage name)
Lesane Parish Crooks (full birth name)
2Pac (stage name)
Makaveli (stage name)

Birth Date: June 16, 1971

Birth Place: Manhattan, New York City, U.S.A.

Date of Death: September 13, 1996


Tupac was an American rapper, actor, and poet known for his powerful lyrics about racism, poverty, violence, and social injustice. He became one of the most influential and iconic figures in hip-hop. Despite his short life, his music and activism left a lasting global impact.


Tupac produced some of the most enduring hits in hip-hop, blending raw emotion with social commentary. Songs like “California Love,” “Changes,” “Hit ’Em Up,” “Dear Mama,” “Keep Ya Head Up,” “Hail Mary,” and “All Eyez on Me” showcased his versatility—from party anthems to deeply personal reflections. His music often addressed themes of inequality, resilience, and loyalty, resonating with listeners across generations. These hits cemented his legacy as one of the most influential artists in rap history.

Snoop Doggy Dogg

Name/s: Snoop Doggy Dogg (full stage name)
Calvin Cordozar Broadus Jr. (full birth name)
Snoop Dogg (stage name)

Birth Date: October 20, 1971

Birth Place: Long Beach, California, U.S.A.

Date of Death: N/A


Snoop Dogg is an American rapper, singer, actor, and cultural icon who rose to fame in the early 1990s under Dr. Dre’s mentorship. Known for his laid-back flow and West Coast sound, he became one of hip-hop’s most recognizable figures. Throughout his long career, he has remained influential in music, pop culture, and entertainment.


Snoop Dogg has delivered some of hip-hop’s most iconic hits, marked by his smooth flow and unmistakable West Coast style. His breakout tracks like “Gin and Juice,” “Who Am I? (What’s My Name?),” and his features on “Nuthin’ but a ‘G’ Thang” and “Still D.R.E.” helped define 1990s rap. Later songs such as “Drop It Like It’s Hot,” “Beautiful,” and “Young, Wild & Free” showed his ability to stay relevant across generations. These hits highlight his versatility and cement his status as one of rap’s most enduring and influential artists.
